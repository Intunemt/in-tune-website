Does this sound like you?

Here are the common problems people bring to music therapy — so you know we're familiar with what you're going through.

Self awareness isn't translating into relief

You're highly aware of what's going wrong in your life, maybe you've even been called an "over-intellectualizer", or have a lot of insight- but you're still stuck.

Talking about it isn't getting to the core of the issue

Words aren't enough- traditional talk therapy doesn't give you the tools tools you actually need. Music can go where words cannot.

Masking is starting to cost you

You feel misunderstood or alienated by traditional therapy. You crave something predictable yet novel to work through the stress of life.

Why Music Therapy?

Embody Your Authentic Self

Music therapy provides a space transcendent to words to explore emotion, identity, and the self, especially when the truth is beyond words.

Regulate Emotions

The path of music through the brain involves the emotion center twice. Music deeply affects emotions.

Increase Self-Awareness

Learn more about your authentic self through music therapy experiences.

Strengthen Relationships

Explore connection with yourself or others through the therapeutic process.

Process Trauma

Access non-verbal pathways to heal safely.

Reduce Stress

This isn't about just listening to a relaxation track. Use your music to get your stress level to where you want it to be.

Increase Focus

Music activates presence and cognitive clarity.
